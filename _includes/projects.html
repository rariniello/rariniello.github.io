Plasma Wakefield Acceleration

My research focuses on demonstrating a plasma wakefield accelerator that meets the demanding parameters required for a future linear collider stage. As co-PI of the E301 experiment at FACET-II, I lead the experimental campaign to achieve these goals using a laser-ionized plasma source.

FACET-II Interaction Point

The interaction point at the end of the kilometer-long FACET accelerator accommodates multiple remotely operated physics experiments. I designed the optical, opto-mechanical, motion, and software control systems for the main experimental chamber. I then built, installed, and commissioned the hardware prior to supporting experimental campaigns that led to several publications.

Ultrafast Laser Upgrades and Operation

The FACET S20 experimental laser was not delivering pulse parameters suitable for several experiments, including my own. I implemented diagnostics to fully characterize the laser pulse and systematically addressed performance-limiting factors. My improvements and upgrades increased laser intensity fivefold and improved uptime to 97%, enabling high-impact science across multiple experimental collaborations.

Tandem Lens Diffractive Optics

A key challenge in plasma wakefield acceleration is generating a plasma with a suitable density profile. I devised a diffractive optics system that enables near-complete control over the density profile of a laser-ionized plasma. This system has been successfully used in multiple experimental campaigns.

Laser-Plasma Simulation Tools

I have developed several tools to model the nonlinear optical process of ionization with a high-power ultrafast laser. These include a Fourier optics pulse propagator, a nonlinear laser-ionized plasma simulation package based on the beam propagation method, and an implementation of the unidirectional pulse propagation equation.

Experimental Control System

  • Qt, PyDM, control systems

Executing a complex laser-ionized plasma wakefield experiment within a single beamtime shift requires speed and efficiency. To make this possible, I wrote a suite of tools for automating experimental procedures, along with several feedback systems to compensate for drifts.
